Regeneration Overview

Passive Regeneration

The passive regeneration process occurs naturally during normal driving conditions when exhaust temperatures are sufficiently high to oxidize and burn off the accumulated soot in the DPF.

  • Most effective on highways with speeds above 55-65 MPH (88-105 Km/h).
  • Requires continuous driving for at least 20-30 minutes to burn off soot.

Active Regeneration Process (ARP)

The Active Regeneration Process is a controlled process initiated by the ECU to burn off accumulated soot in the DPF when passive regeneration conditions are not met or soot levels are too high. Failure to do this may result in:

  • Engine power reduction
  • Increased fuel consumption
  • Additional warning lights
  • Potential damage to DPF and engine

Active Regeneration - When to Do It and How to Do It

When to Initiate: Start an Active Regeneration Process when the DPF lamp is flashing.

The 5-Step Process

  1. Prepare the Vehicle: Park in a safe area (away from vegetation, structures or flammable materials), set transmission to Neutral, engage Parking Brake.
  2. Warm the Engine: Start and warm up the engine until the coolant temperature reaches 150°F (66°C).
  3. Set Engine and Transmission:
    • Manual Truck: Keep at slow idle
    • Automatic Truck: Briefly shift to Gear, then back to Neutral
  4. Initiate Regeneration: Lift the Regen Switch Guard and hold the DPF button for 5 seconds until the HEST lamp lights up.
  5. Monitor and Complete:
    • Manual Truck: Stay within the vehicle, Engine speed increases, DPF lamp turns off.
    • Automatic Truck: Remain in vehicle; regeneration completes in 20-60 minutes.

Stopping Regeneration

  • Automatic Transmission: Press the clutch, brake, or accelerator pedal. Press and hold the Regen Inhibit Switch until idle returns to normal. Shut down engine if needed.
  • Manual Transmission: Press and hold the Regen Inhibit Switch until idle returns to normal. Shut down engine if needed.
