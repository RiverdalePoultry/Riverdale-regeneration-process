Regeneration Process Overview

Origin Date: Nov 8, 2024

Approved by: Operations Department

Regeneration Overview (Passive vs Active)

Passive Regeneration Process: Occurs naturally during driving when exhaust temperatures are high enough to burn off soot in the Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF).

  • Most effective on highways at speeds above 55-65 MPH (88-105 Km/h).
  • Continuous driving for 20-30 minutes helps burn off soot.

Active Regeneration Process (ARP)

Triggered by the ECU when passive conditions aren’t met, or soot levels are too high, usually at ~70-90% soot accumulation.

  • Risks of not doing an ARP include engine power reduction, increased fuel consumption, warning lights, and potential damage to DPF and engine.

Active Regeneration - When and How

When to Do It: When the DPF lamp is flashing.

5-Step Process:

  1. Park in a safe area, set transmission to Neutral, and engage Parking Brake.
  2. Warm the engine until coolant temperature reaches 150°F (66°C).
  3. Set Engine and Transmission:
    • Manual: Keep at slow idle
    • Automatic: Briefly shift to Gear, then back to Neutral
  4. Initiate Regeneration: Lift the Regen Switch Guard and hold the DPF button for 5 seconds.
  5. Monitor and Complete:
    • Manual: Engine speed increases, DPF lamp turns off.
    • Automatic: Stay within vehicle; process finishes in 20-60 minutes.

Stopping Regeneration

  • Automatic: Press clutch, brake, or accelerator pedal, or hold Regen Inhibit Switch.
  • Manual: Hold Regen Inhibit Switch until idle normalizes.
